This page describes how I handle vulnerabilities I discover in third-party software and systems.

## Principles

I follow a coordinated disclosure model. When I discover a vulnerability I contact the affected vendor privately, give them reasonable time to patch, and disclose publicly after that window has passed — regardless of whether a fix exists.

## Timeline

**90 days** from the date of initial vendor notification to public disclosure.

If a patch is released before the 90-day window closes, I will publish the writeup shortly after the patch is publicly available. If the vendor is unresponsive or disputes the report, I reserve the right to disclose at 90 days with or without a fix.

Extensions may be granted on a case-by-case basis if a patch is actively in progress and a firm release date is communicated.

## Scope

This policy applies to vulnerabilities I discover independently through my own research. It does not apply to vulnerabilities discovered under a signed engagement agreement, bug bounty program rules, or other contractual obligations — those are governed by the terms of the relevant program.

## Contacting Me

If you believe you have found a vulnerability in something I maintain, or want to reach me regarding a report, use the contact details on the [About](/about/) page. Encrypted communication is strongly preferred.

## Out of Scope

I do not accept reports for issues I did not discover. I am not a bug bounty triage service.
